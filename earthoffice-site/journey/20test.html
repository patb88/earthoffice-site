3. Putting this together with the estradiol curve

From the estradiol side (using the fixed ER model we set up):

  • ER occupancy went from ~32% (32 pg/mL) → ~57% (89 pg/mL) → projected ~60–75% as you stabilize around 100–200 pg/mL.
  • AR occupancy is going in the opposite direction: ~42% → ~35% → ~22% → ~12% → ~6–7%.

That gives a nice combined picture:

  • Early months:
    • ER occupancy crossing its midpoint (Kd region).
    • AR occupancy still substantial.
      → “Mixed-mode” brain: both estrogenic and androgenic tones present; big shifts, but not fully settled.
  • 6–12 months:
    • ER occupancy in the 60–70% band.
    • AR occupancy slipping toward 20% → 10%.
      → Estrogen becomes the dominant scriptwriter for limbic and affective patterns; androgens become more of a background hum.
  • 12–24 months:
    • ER high and relatively stable.
    • AR in low single digits to ~10%.
      → You’re living in an estrogen-dominant, low-androgen brain environment; most of the qualitative shifts are now about network remodeling and identity integration, not about large receptor-level swings.
