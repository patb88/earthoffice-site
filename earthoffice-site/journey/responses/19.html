Here’s a breakdown of the what, why, when, and how of hot flashes in the context of feminizing hormone therapy:

🔥 What Are Hot Flashes?

A hot flash is a sudden, often intense feeling of warmth — usually in the upper body — often accompanied by:

  • Skin flushing (red face/neck)
  • Sweating (especially on the face or chest)
  • Heart pounding or lightheadedness
  • Chills or "cold flash" afterward
  • Irritability, brain fog, or fatigue

Duration can range from a few seconds to 10–15 minutes, and they often occur in waves — especially at night (called night sweats).

🧬 Why Do They Happen in AMAB HRT?

Hot flashes are caused by hormonal fluctuations, especially:

  • Rapid drops in testosterone
  • Insufficient estrogen levels to stabilize the hypothalamus, the brain’s internal thermostat

So, in AMAB people on feminizing HRT, hot flashes are most likely when:

  • Testosterone is suppressed quickly (e.g. from spironolactone or orchiectomy)
  • Estradiol is not yet at a stable or sufficient level to fully replace the hormonal signaling
  • Estradiol levels dip due to missed doses, patch wear-off, or poor oral absorption
