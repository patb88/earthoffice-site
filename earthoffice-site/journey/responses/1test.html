Definition and Brief of Gender Dysphoria

Gender dysphoria is a clinical term used to describe the significant psychological distress or impairment that arises when an individual's gender identity—their internal sense of being male, female, both, neither, or somewhere along the spectrum—does not align with the sex they were assigned at birth.

According to the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition, Text Revision (DSM-5-TR), gender dysphoria is diagnosed when this incongruence causes clinically significant distress or functional impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of life.

Key components of DSM-5-TR criteria include:

  • A marked incongruence between one’s experienced/expressed gender and assigned gender.
  • A desire to be rid of or avoid primary and/or secondary sex characteristics.
  • A desire to have the physical traits and social roles of another gender.
  • Symptoms persist for at least 6 months.
  • Significant distress or impairment in functioning is present.

It’s important to note that gender nonconformity alone is not gender dysphoria. Dysphoria specifically refers to the distress caused by this incongruence—not the incongruence itself.
